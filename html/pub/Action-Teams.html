National Partnership to End Interpersonal Violence Across The Lifespan

1. Public Awareness

This Action Team develops a national public awareness surrounding the issues of interpersonal violence.


Tasks for team members include:
1. Developing webinar series to publicize NPEIV
2. Engaging in a larger media campaign “Abuse Stops Here.”
3. Researching and contacting potential speakers to educate the public in areas of violence.﻿

2. Training & Mentoring

This Action Team helps to create educational opportunities and mentoring.
Tasks for team members include:
1. Identifying various areas in which additional training and mentoring could increase professional competence regarding violence

2. Identifying ways to raise awareness about the necessity of ending interpersonal violence
3. Planning events designed to facilitate an opportunity for action teams members to increase their knowledge of interpersonal violence and related issues.

3. Practice

This Action Team focuses on increasing the evidence-based knowledge available regarding best practices and standards of care for competent intervention services.


Tasks for team members include:
1. Compiling an online clearinghouse of references regarding best practices and standards of care

2. Developing guidelines on NPEIV’s point of view on best practices and standards of care

3. Collecting information on clinicians directly involved in treating trauma to bolster the accessibility to referrals for competent care nationwide.

4. Research

This Action Team focuses on the promotion of research in the areas of violence, integrating practice issues into research, and supporting basic and applied research.


Tasks for team members include:
1. Compiling a list of relevant research clearinghouses

2. Listing research as related to family courts, statute of limitations, and others
3. Actively engaging action team members to seek out new forms of communication and dissemination possibilities for research.

5. Public Policy

This Action Team focuses on coordinating education efforts on policy impact, identifying needs and developing Points of View directly involved with policy with respect to reducing and eliminating interpersonal violence and abuse. 


Tasks for this action team include:
1. Policy statements will be developed concerning our main areas and topics of interest that correspond to NPEIV priorities and strategies. 
2. Congressional briefings concerning these efforts can be coordinated by this action team to educate congress and other policy makers at state and federal levels.
3. Compiling a data base of existing policies at state and federal levels as needed that directly affect interpersonal violence.﻿

6. ﻿﻿Dissemination & Translation

This Action Team focuses on developing strategies and processes for making basic and applied scientific study of interpersonal aggression accessible to all advocates, clinicians, and others who have limited research backgrounds.


Tasks for team members include:

1. Facilitating the application of research developments to clinical practice
2. Enhancing researcher-clinician dialogue

3. Developing a web-based publication (Science to Service Digest) designed to provide lay language summaries of scientific research on forms of interpersonal violence.
